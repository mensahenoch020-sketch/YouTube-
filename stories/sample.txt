They say the old lighthouse on Miller's Point has been empty for thirty years.
But every night at exactly three a.m., the light still turns on.
Last winter, a fisherman named Coyle decided to find out why.
He climbed the rusted stairs to the very top, his flashlight trembling in his hand.
When he reached the lamp room, he froze. The bulb was shattered. Completely dark.
Yet the beam outside swept across the water, bright and steady.
Behind him, a voice whispered: "You're not supposed to be here."
Coyle never went back to the sea again. And the light? It still turns on every night.
